Stonewall

The Stonewall Uprising

In the early morning hours of June 28, 1969, a riot broke out in response to a police raid on the Stonewall Inn, at the time one of the City’s best known LGBT bars. Over the course of the next several days, more demonstrations and riots occurred in the surrounding neighborhood including Christopher Park. At its peak, the crowds included several thousand people.

The Stonewall Uprising is regarded by many as the single most important event that led to the development of the modern LGBT civil rights movement. The uprising inspired LGBT people throughout the country to organize and within two years of Stonewall, LGBT rights groups had been started in nearly every major city in the U.S.

On June 24, 2016, President Barack Obama signed a Presidential Proclamation establishing the Stonewall National Monument.

“Stonewall will be our first national monument to tell the story of the struggle for LGBT rights. I believe our national parks should reflect the full story of our country – the richness and diversity and uniquely American spirit that has always defined us. That we are stronger together. That out of many, we are one.”

President Barack Obama
